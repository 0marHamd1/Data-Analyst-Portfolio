## 🧾 Employee Attrition Analysis

---

## 🔹 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee data to understand the key factors driving attrition and identify areas where the company can improve employee retention.

The goal is to uncover patterns behind employee turnover and support data-driven HR decisions.

---

## 🔹 Dataset

- Source: IBM HR Employee Attrition Dataset
- Type: Employee records
- Key fields: Attrition, Department, Salary, OverTime, Years at Company

---

## 🔹 Tools Used

- Excel → Data validation
- Power BI → Data modeling, DAX, and dashboard creation

---

## 🔹 Key Metrics (KPIs)

- Total Employees
- Employees Left
- Attrition Rate

---

# Key Insights

## Department Attrition

Sales department shows the highest attrition rate compared to other departments.

---

## Overtime Impact

Employees working overtime have significantly higher attrition rates than those who do not.

---

## Salary Effect

Employees with lower salaries (0–5000) have the highest attrition rates, followed by the 5000–10000 group.

---

## Early Attrition Pattern

Most employees leave within the first two years, indicating early-stage retention issues.

---

## Outlier Observation

A spike in attrition at 23 years appears to be an outlier due to a very small sample size.

---

# Business Recommendations

## Improve Sales Department Retention

Investigate working conditions, targets, and incentives in the Sales department to reduce high attrition rates.

---

## Reduce Excessive Overtime

Implement better workload distribution and limit excessive overtime to improve work-life balance.

---

## Review Salary Structure

Adjust compensation for lower-income employees to improve satisfaction and retention.

---

## Strengthen Onboarding Programs

Enhance onboarding and early employee engagement to reduce attrition within the first two years.

---

## Focus on Retention Strategy

Shift HR strategy from reactive hiring to proactive employee retention.

---

# Dashboard Features

- KPI Cards (Employees, Attrition Rate)
- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Overtime
- Attrition by Salary Group
- Attrition by Years at Company

---

## Conclusion

Employee attrition is primarily driven by overtime, low salaries, and early-stage dissatisfaction. By addressing these factors, the company can significantly improve employee retention and reduce turnover costs.
